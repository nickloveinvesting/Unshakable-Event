# Section 9: Crisis Playbook - Visual Placement Guide

## Overview
This document specifies all visual elements, callout boxes, worksheets, and design markers for Section 9: Crisis Playbook.

---

## Section Design Specifications

**Section Accent Color:** Dark Gray to Bright Gold Gradient (#2C3E50 → #D4AF37)
- Symbolism: Phoenix rising from darkness into light; rebirth and resilience
- Supporting Colors: Red (#E74C3C) for urgency/crisis, Green (#27AE60) for recovery/growth

**Section Tone:** Dark opening (2008 story) transitioning to hopeful ending (comeback and legacy)

**Typography:**
- Main text: Same as sections 1-8 (maintain consistency)
- Callout boxes: Bold, slightly larger for emphasis
- Quotes: Toby's voice, italicized, attributed

## Worksheet Designs

### WORKSHEET 9.1: Crisis Risk Matrix
**Location:** After Exercise 9.1

**Format:** Full page (8.5" × 11")

**Structure:**
- Grid with 5 rows (one per crisis type)
- Columns: Crisis Type | Likelihood (1-5) | Impact (1-5) | Total Risk Score | Early Warnings | Mitigation Plan

**Design Elements:**
- Color coding:
  - Likelihood/Impact cells: 1-3 = Green, 3-4 = Yellow, 5 = Red
  - High-risk items highlighted in red
  - Heat map coloring to visualize risk profile
- Worksheet instructions at top
- Examples provided in gray italics
- Space for notes and additional risks at bottom

**Key Sections:**
1. Header: "My Crisis Risk Assessment"
2. Instructions (3-4 bullet points)
3. Main grid (5 crisis types)
4. Risk scoring guide (1-25 scale)
5. Reflection questions at bottom
6. Space for quarterly review dates

---

### WORKSHEET 9.2: Crisis Response Playbook
**Location:** After Exercise 9.2

**Format:** 2-page spread (8.5" × 11" landscape)

**Page 1: First 72 Hours**
- Header: "STOP. ASSESS. STABILIZE."
- Three columns:
  - Hour 1-24 (STOP)
  - Hour 24-48 (ASSESS)
  - Hour 48-72 (STABILIZE)
- Within each column:
  - Key questions to ask
  - Actions to take
  - People to contact
  - Decisions to make
- Checkboxes for tracking completion

**Page 2: First 30 Days**
- Four columns for weeks 1-4
- Each week shows:
  - Primary goal (Triage, Communicate, Execute, Review)
  - Key actions
  - Who to involve
  - Progress checkpoints
- At bottom: Support system contact info boxes

**Design Elements:**
- Color coding: Red (STOP) → Yellow (ASSESS) → Green (STABILIZE)
- Progress tracker at bottom ("How far along are we?")
- Emergency contact section with fields for:
  - Spouse/partner phone
  - Accountant/financial advisor phone
  - Lawyer phone
  - Mentor/coach phone
  - Therapist phone

### WORKSHEET 9.3: Business Continuity Playbook
**Location:** After Exercise 9.3

**Format:** 2-page spread (8.5" × 11" landscape)

**Page 1: Expense Audit & Triage**
- Header: "What Lives? What Dies?"
- Three-column format:
  - Expense category (from your Profit First)
  - Monthly cost
  - Tier (1, 2, or 3)
- Pre-filled categories:
  - Payroll
  - Rent/Facilities
  - Utilities
  - Insurance
  - Debt service
  - Software
  - Marketing
  - Travel
  - [Space for custom items]
- Subtotal row for each tier
- Total savings calculation
- Visual: 3-tier savings calculator (starting point → Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3)

**Page 2: Reserve Strategy**
- Header: "How Long Can We Survive?"
- Calculator section:
  - Monthly operating expenses: $_____
  - Current cash reserves: $_____
  - Months of runway: _____ months
- Target reserve section:
  - Choose target: 3 months / 6 months / 12 months
  - Funding plan:
    - % of profit going to reserves
    - Target completion date
    - Monthly amount needed
- Visual: Reserve growth timeline
- Core vs. non-core business analysis:
  - Revenue generators (must keep)
  - Support functions (can cut)
  - Nice-to-haves (eliminate first)

## Page Break Specifications

- **Major section breaks:** Full page break with visual marker
- **Sub-section breaks:** Half-page visual marker if space allows
- **Exercise/Worksheet breaks:** Always on new page

---

## Design Consistency with Sections 1-8

- Use same fonts as previous sections
- Maintain consistent icon style across all visuals
- Color palette should complement but not conflict with earlier sections
- Callout box style consistent with sections 1-8
- Worksheet templates match previous section design

---

## Accessibility Considerations

- All visuals have alt text descriptions
- Color-coded elements also have pattern/texture differentiation
- Large enough fonts for readability (minimum 11pt body, 14pt headings)
- High contrast for callout boxes
- Sufficient white space to prevent visual overwhelm

---

*This visual guide ensures Section 9 is both emotionally powerful and practically clear, using visual design to reinforce the workbook's transformation journey.*
