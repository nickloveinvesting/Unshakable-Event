## Key Research Findings Summary

### 1. Neuroscience of Identity (neuroscience-identity-transformation.md)
- The medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) is the brain's "identity center"
- Neuroplasticity enables identity change at any age
- 1 hour of daily focus can double neural connections (Dispenza)
- Mental rehearsal creates same brain changes as physical experience
- Key researchers: Dispenza, Barrett, Huberman

### 2. CBT for Identity Change (cbt-frameworks-identity-change.md)
- Core beliefs literally ARE identity (formed in childhood)
- CBT has massive evidence base: 409 trials, 52,702 patients, g=0.79 effect size
- 10 cognitive distortions predictably block identity growth
- Thought records and behavioral experiments change beliefs
- Key researchers: Aaron Beck, Judith Beck, David Burns

### 3. Psychological Archetypes (psychological-archetypes-leadership.md)
- 12 archetypes map complete journey of human development
- Shadow work prevents self-sabotage in leadership
- IBM and major corporations use archetype systems
- PMAI assessment provides validated archetype identification
- Key researchers: Jung, Carol Pearson, Hugh Marr

### 4. Narrative Identity (narrative-identity-story-reconstruction.md)
- "We are the stories we tell about ourselves" - McAdams
- Redemptive stories predict well-being; contamination stories predict depression
- Generative adults tell more redemptive stories
- Stories can be rewritten while respecting facts
- Key researchers: Dan McAdams, Kate McLean

### 5. Self-Assessment Tools (self-assessment-tools-identity-clarity.md)
- Self-concept clarity (SCC) predicts life satisfaction and meaning
- Big Five is "gold standard" with 735+ publications in 5 years
- NISE (2024) is first validated narrative identity self-report
- Multiple assessments provide fuller picture than any single tool
- Assessment enables transformation by creating awareness baseline
