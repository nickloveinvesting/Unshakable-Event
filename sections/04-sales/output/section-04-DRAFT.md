## INTRODUCTION: THE MORTGAGE BROKER WHO DIDN'T PUSH

Early in my mortgage career, I watched other brokers use every trick in the book.

High-pressure closes. "This rate won't last—sign today or lose it forever." (It would last. They were lying.)

Hidden fees buried in paperwork. Clients would sign without understanding what they were paying for.

Pushing people into loans they couldn't afford. Bigger loan meant bigger commission. Who cared if they could actually make the payments?

And here's the thing: **it worked.** Short term. Those brokers closed deals. They made money. They hit their numbers.

But I watched something else happen too. I watched their clients come back angry. I watched referrals dry up. I watched reputations crumble. I watched businesses built on manipulation collapse when the market shifted and they had no loyal customers to sustain them.

I decided early: I wasn't going to build like that.

### The Deal That Changed Everything

There was a couple—I still remember them—who came to me wanting to buy more house than they could afford. They'd been pre-approved by another lender for a loan that would have consumed 55% of their monthly income. On paper, they qualified. In reality, they'd be house-poor within a year.

The other broker would have closed that deal. Easy commission.

I sat them down and showed them the math. I showed them what their life would look like with that payment—the vacations they couldn't take, the retirement they couldn't fund, the stress they'd carry every month. I recommended a smaller loan. A smaller house. A smaller commission for me.

They were confused at first. "You're talking yourself out of money."

"I'm talking you into a life you can actually live," I said. "I'd rather you buy a house you love living in than a house that owns you."

They bought the smaller house. My commission was about 40% less than it could have been.

Here's what happened next: Over the following three years, that couple sent me **fourteen referrals.** Friends, family, coworkers—anyone they knew who needed a mortgage, they sent to "the guy who talked us out of making a huge mistake."

Those fourteen referrals generated more revenue than twenty high-pressure closes would have. And every single one of those clients trusted me from the first conversation because they'd heard the story of how I operated.

### The Lesson That Built Imperial Mortgage

That experience taught me something those other brokers never learned:

**When you solve people's real problems—even when it costs you short-term—they don't just buy once. They buy forever. And they send everyone they know.**

I built Imperial Mortgage on that principle. Not manipulation. Not pressure. Not tricks. Problem-solving. Listening until I understood their situation better than they did. Then showing them the path forward.

Was it slower at first? Yes. Did I leave money on the table sometimes? Yes. Did I build something that lasted while the manipulators burned out? Absolutely.

The integrity-first approach isn't just more ethical. It's more profitable. It just requires patience and playing the long game.

That's what I'm going to teach you in this section.

## PART 1: SALES PSYCHOLOGY & THE 7 PRINCIPLES OF INFLUENCE

Before we get into tactics and scripts, you need to understand WHY people buy. Not surface-level why. Deep, neurological why.

Here's the research that will change how you think about sales: **95% of purchase decisions are made by the subconscious mind before the logical brain even engages.** Harvard Business School professor Gerald Zaltman proved this through decades of consumer neuroscience research.

What does that mean for you? It means logical arguments alone don't close deals. Features and benefits alone don't close deals. The emotional brain decides first, then the logical brain justifies. Your job is to connect with BOTH—in the right order.

### The Triune Brain in Sales

Your prospect's brain has three layers, and each plays a role in buying:

| Brain Region | Function | What It's Asking |
|--------------|----------|------------------|
| **Reptilian Brain** | Survival, safety | "Is this safe? Will this hurt me?" |
| **Limbic Brain** | Emotions, memories, trust | "Do I like this? Does it feel right?" |
| **Neocortex** | Logic, analysis, language | "Does this make sense? Can I justify it?" |

**Critical insight:** The emotional limbic brain activates about 3 seconds BEFORE the logical neocortex during purchase decisions. The decision is made emotionally first. Logic comes second to rationalize what emotion already decided.

This is why manipulation works short-term—it hijacks the emotional brain. And it's why integrity works long-term—genuine care creates positive emotional associations that manipulation never can.

### Cialdini's 7 Principles of Influence

Dr. Robert Cialdini spent decades researching what makes people say yes. His seven principles aren't manipulation tactics—they're descriptions of how human psychology works. You can use them ethically to help people make good decisions, or unethically to exploit them. The principles are neutral; your intent determines the ethics.

**Principle 1: Reciprocity**
People feel obligated to return favors. When you give first, they want to give back.

*Ethical application:* Provide genuine value before asking for anything. Free consultations. Useful content. Helpful advice even when they don't buy. When you've already helped them, asking for the sale feels natural.

*Manipulation warning:* Don't give fake "gifts" designed solely to create obligation. People can sense the difference.

**Principle 2: Commitment & Consistency**
People want to be consistent with what they've already said or done. Small yeses lead to bigger yeses.

*Ethical application:* Help prospects make small commitments that align with their stated goals. "You mentioned you want to grow revenue by 20% this year. If this solution delivered that, would it be worth exploring?"

*Manipulation warning:* Don't trick people into commitments they don't mean. That's bait-and-switch.

**Principle 3: Social Proof**
People look to others—especially similar others—to determine correct behavior. "People like me do this."

*Ethical application:* Share genuine testimonials and case studies from clients similar to your prospect. "Companies in your industry have seen results like..."

*Manipulation warning:* Never fabricate testimonials or inflate results. It always comes out.

**Principle 4: Authority**
People trust experts and defer to credible sources. Expertise creates influence.

*Ethical application:* Demonstrate real knowledge. Share credentials that matter. Let your expertise show through the quality of your questions and insights.

*Manipulation warning:* Don't fake credentials or expertise you don't have. Build real authority instead.

**Principle 5: Liking**
People say yes to people they like. Rapport matters.

*Ethical application:* Find genuine common ground. Be authentically interested in them. Build real relationships, not fake friendliness.

*Manipulation warning:* Don't manufacture false similarities or fake enthusiasm. Authenticity beats technique.

**Principle 6: Scarcity**
People want what's limited or becoming unavailable. Scarcity creates urgency.

*Ethical application:* If there's genuine scarcity—limited spots, real deadline, actual price increase—communicate it clearly.

*Manipulation warning:* NEVER create fake scarcity. "Only 3 left!" when you have 300 destroys trust instantly and permanently.

**Principle 7: Unity**
People say yes to those who share their identity. "You're one of us."

*Ethical application:* Find genuine shared identity—same industry, same challenges, same values. "As fellow business owners, we both understand..."

*Manipulation warning:* Don't fake belonging to groups you're not part of.

### The Ethics of Influence

Here's the line between ethical influence and manipulation:

**Ethical influence:** Using these principles to help someone make a decision that's genuinely good for them.

**Manipulation:** Using these principles to get someone to do something that benefits you at their expense.

The test is simple: **Do you actually believe what you're selling helps them?**

If yes—you're persuading. You're using influence to help them overcome inertia and make a good decision.

If no—you're manipulating. And manipulation always catches up with you.

### The 5 Archetype Sales Approaches

Based on the most common entrepreneurial archetypes, here's how each naturally sells:

**THE RULER (Authority-Based Selling)**

| Strength | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Commands respect | Leads with expertise and vision | High-ticket, complex sales |
| Decisive presence | "Here's what you need to do" | Executive-level conversations |
| Strategic thinking | Paints picture of outcomes | Consulting, advisory services |

*Natural approach:* Rulers don't beg or persuade. They present the opportunity and let the prospect decide if they want to be part of it. They sell by demonstrating they're the obvious expert choice.

*Watch out for:* Coming across as arrogant or dismissive. Rulers need to balance authority with genuine curiosity about the prospect's situation.

**THE CREATOR (Innovation-Based Selling)**

| Strength | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Visionary thinking | Leads with possibility | Disruptive products, new markets |
| Enthusiasm | "Imagine what this could become" | Creative services |
| Original perspective | Paints transformation pictures | Innovation-driven sales |

*Natural approach:* Creators sell by helping prospects see possibilities they couldn't see before. They paint vivid pictures of what could be and generate excitement about the future.

*Watch out for:* Getting lost in vision without addressing practical concerns. Creators need to ground their enthusiasm in concrete results.

**THE SAGE (Wisdom-Based Selling)**

| Strength | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Deep knowledge | Leads with education | Consulting, coaching, advisory |
| Analytical insight | "Let me teach you something" | Complex B2B sales |
| Thoughtful questions | Helps them understand their problem | Professional services |

*Natural approach:* Sages sell by teaching. They share insights that help prospects understand their situation more clearly, then offer solutions. The sale emerges from the education.

*Watch out for:* Over-teaching without ever asking for the sale. Sages need to remember that at some point, the lesson needs to lead to action.

**THE WARRIOR (Results-Based Selling)**

| Strength | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Direct communication | Leads with results | Performance-based offerings |
| Competitive energy | "Here's what I achieved, you can too" | Training, coaching |
| Action orientation | Challenges them to act | Sales where urgency matters |

*Natural approach:* Warriors sell through demonstration of results. They share their track record, challenge the prospect's current approach, and push for decisive action.

*Watch out for:* Being too aggressive or making prospects feel attacked. Warriors need to channel their competitive energy into supporting the prospect, not overwhelming them.

**THE CAREGIVER (Service-Based Selling)**

| Strength | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Genuine empathy | Leads with understanding | Service businesses |
| Support orientation | "I'm here to help you" | Relationship-based sales |
| Patient listening | Takes time to understand deeply | Long-term client relationships |

*Natural approach:* Caregivers sell by demonstrating they care more about the prospect's success than their own commission. The sale emerges from trust built through genuine service.

*Watch out for:* Being too accommodating and never asking for the business. Caregivers need to remember that serving the prospect includes helping them make decisions.

## PART 3: OBJECTION HANDLING FRAMEWORKS

Here's a mindset shift that will transform how you experience sales:

**Objections aren't rejections. They're questions.**

When someone says "It's too expensive," they're not saying "Go away." They're saying "I don't understand the value yet." When someone says "I need to think about it," they're not saying "No." They're saying "I have a concern I haven't voiced."

Research shows that 60% of customers say "no" four times before saying "yes." And salespeople who effectively handle objections are 2.4 times more likely to meet their quota.

The difference between struggling and succeeding often comes down to how you respond when you hear "but..."

### The Psychology Behind Objections

Objections come from three psychological states—Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt (FUD):

| State | What They're Feeling | What They Need |
|-------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Fear** | "What if this goes wrong?" | Safety, risk reversal, guarantees |
| **Uncertainty** | "I don't have enough information" | Clarity, specifics, proof |
| **Doubt** | "I've been burned before" | Trust, credibility, references |

Your job isn't to "overcome" their objections. Your job is to **understand and address** the underlying concern. When you do that, the objection dissolves.

### The 5 Most Common Objections (And What They Really Mean)

**"It's too expensive"**
*What they're really asking:* "I don't see how the value exceeds the price."
*What they need:* Help calculating the true cost of their problem vs. your solution

**"I need to think about it"**
*What they're really feeling:* Fear of making the wrong decision
*What they need:* Help identifying specifically what concerns them

**"I need to talk to my spouse/partner/team"**
*What they're really saying:* "I'm not the only decision-maker" OR "I need help selling this internally"
*What they need:* Materials to share OR direct access to the other stakeholders

**"Can you send me more information?"**
*What they're really doing:* Politely ending the conversation
*What they need:* A reason to stay engaged OR clarification on what specific information would help

**"The timing isn't right"**
*What they're really saying:* "This isn't a priority" OR "I'm using timing as a polite rejection"
*What they need:* Understanding of the cost of waiting OR permission to reconnect later

### The 3-Step Objection Response Formula

When you hear an objection, don't react. Respond with this sequence:

**Step 1: ACKNOWLEDGE**
Validate their concern. Don't dismiss it.

"I understand." / "That's a fair concern." / "I hear you."

**Step 2: CLARIFY**
Ask a question to understand the real concern underneath.

"Help me understand—when you say 'expensive,' what are you comparing it to?"
"What specifically would you need to think through?"

**Step 3: RESPOND**
Address the actual concern with relevant information or reframe.

"Here's another way to look at it..." / "What we've found is..." / "If I could show you..."

### Objection Response Scripts

**"It's too expensive"**

*Acknowledge:* "I get it. This is a real investment, and you want to make sure it's worth it."

*Clarify:* "Help me understand—is it that the price is higher than you expected, or that you're not sure the return justifies the cost?"

*Respond (if value issue):* "Let me ask you this: what is this problem currently costing you? If you factor in [their specific pain points], what's the monthly cost of NOT solving it? Most of our clients find that when they do that math, the investment pays for itself within [timeframe]."

*Respond (if budget issue):* "I understand budget constraints are real. If budget weren't a factor, would this be the right solution? [If yes] Then let's talk about how to make this work—payment plans, phased implementation, or starting smaller and expanding."

**"I need to think about it"**

*Acknowledge:* "Of course. Important decisions deserve careful thought."

*Clarify:* "So I can help you think it through—what specifically do you need to consider? Is it the fit, the timing, the investment, or something else?"

*Respond:* [Address whatever they identify] "If I could address [their concern] right now, would you be ready to move forward?"

**"I need to talk to my partner/team"**

*Acknowledge:* "Absolutely. It's smart to include the people this affects."

*Clarify:* "Help me understand the best way to support that conversation. What questions do you think they'll have? What concerns might they raise?"

*Respond:* "Would it be helpful if I prepared materials specifically addressing those points? Or even better—would it make sense for me to join that conversation so I can answer questions directly?"

## PART 4: RELATIONSHIP-BASED SELLING & CLOSING

Most sales training focuses on "closing techniques"—verbal tricks to get someone to say yes. That approach treats sales as a battle where you're trying to defeat the prospect's resistance.

There's a better way: **Help them buy instead of trying to sell them.**

### The Sandler Principle: Qualify, Don't Convince

David Sandler revolutionized sales with a simple insight: **Stop trying to convince everyone to buy. Start qualifying who's a fit.**

Most salespeople try to CONVINCE everyone. "Let me tell you why you need this..." That's exhausting and ineffective.

Top salespeople QUALIFY. "Let me understand if this is right for you..." They ask questions to determine fit. If someone isn't a fit, they say so. If someone IS a fit, the sale becomes natural.

The Sandler approach:
1. Build genuine rapport (connection before pitch)
2. Set clear expectations (what happens in this conversation)
3. Uncover real pain (what's actually bothering them)
4. Discuss budget early (can they afford a solution?)
5. Map their decision process (who else is involved? what's the timeline?)
6. THEN present your solution (matched to their specific situation)

**Key insight:** Discussing budget EARLY saves everyone time. If they can't afford your solution, better to know upfront than after you've spent an hour presenting.

### The Pain Funnel

The Sandler Pain Funnel is a questioning technique that moves from surface symptoms to emotional core:

```
"Tell me more about that..."
         ↓
"Can you be more specific?"
         ↓
"How long has this been a problem?"
         ↓
"What have you tried to do about it?"
         ↓
"Did that work? Why not?"
         ↓
"How much do you think this has cost you?"
         ↓
"How do you feel about that?" [EMOTIONAL CORE]
```

When you reach the emotional core—how the problem FEELS, not just what it costs—you've reached the real motivation for change.

### Closing Techniques That Feel Like Service

Closing should feel like the natural conclusion of a good conversation, not a battle to win. Here are approaches that maintain integrity:

**The Assumptive Close**
Proceed as if agreement is already reached.

"So, when would you like to get started?"
"Who on your team should be in the kickoff meeting?"
"Should we schedule implementation for next week or the week after?"

*Use when:* You've addressed all concerns, got positive buying signals, and the prospect seems ready. The assumptive close just confirms what's already decided.

*Don't use when:* Significant concerns remain unaddressed. That's pressure, not service.

**The Trial Close**
Test readiness without asking for full commitment.

"If I could solve [specific problem], would you be ready to move forward?"
"Does this approach make sense for your situation?"
"On a scale of 1-10, how close are we to working together? What would make it a 10?"

*Use when:* You're not sure where they stand. Trial closes reveal concerns you might have missed.

**The Alternative Close**
Offer two positive options rather than yes/no.

"Would you prefer to start with the monthly or annual plan?"
"Should we begin implementation this month or next?"
"Do you want the standard package or the premium?"

*Use when:* The prospect is ready but facing decision fatigue. This simplifies their choice while assuming they're moving forward.

**The Summary Close**
Recap all value before asking for decision.

"Let me make sure I have this right. You're dealing with [pain point], which is costing you [impact]. We've agreed that [solution] addresses that by [benefits]. You mentioned the ROI looks strong. Is there anything else you need to make a decision?"

*Use when:* Complex sales where you need to reconnect all the dots before asking for commitment.

**The Urgency Close (ETHICAL VERSION)**
Create time-based motivation—ONLY if real.

"This pricing is guaranteed through Friday. After that, I genuinely can't hold it."
"We have capacity for two more clients this quarter. After that, there's a waitlist."

*Use when:* There's GENUINE scarcity or deadline. NEVER manufacture fake urgency.

## PART 5: SALES PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

Here's a truth that separates amateur salespeople from professionals:

**What gets measured gets managed. What gets managed gets improved.**

If you're not tracking your sales activity systematically, you're guessing. You're hoping. You're leaving your income to chance.

Professionals track. They know their numbers. They can predict their revenue because they understand their pipeline.

### The Sales Funnel: Understanding the Stages

Every sale moves through stages. Understanding these stages helps you know where to focus:

| Stage | Description | Your Job |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Lead** | Someone who might be interested | Generate, capture |
| **Qualified** | Confirmed fit, need, and budget | Discover, assess |
| **Meeting/Demo** | Presentation of your solution | Demonstrate value |
| **Proposal** | Formal offer presented | Handle objections |
| **Negotiation** | Working out final terms | Close with integrity |
| **Closed Won** | Deal completed | Deliver, get referrals |
| **Closed Lost** | Deal didn't happen | Learn, move on |

[VISUAL MARKER: Sales Funnel diagram with conversion rates at each stage]

### Key Metrics You Must Track

**Pipeline Value:** Total value of all active opportunities
- Target: 3x your revenue goal (if you want $100K, maintain $300K in pipeline)

**Conversion Rate:** Percentage moving from each stage to the next
- Industry average: 20-25% win rate
- Top performers: 35-45% win rate

**Sales Cycle Length:** Average time from first contact to close
- Know your average so you can forecast accurately

**Activities Per Day:** Calls, emails, meetings
- This is the leading indicator you control

**Lead Response Time:** How fast you follow up
- Research: Responding within 5 minutes makes you 9x more likely to convert than waiting 30+ minutes

### The Daily Sales Disciplines

Remember Section 2? Daily disciplines compound. Here's how that applies to sales:

**Daily Prospecting (30 minutes)**
- New outreach to potential clients
- Not optional. Not "when you have time." Daily.

**Daily Follow-Up (30 minutes)**
- Move existing pipeline forward
- The fortune is in the follow-up

**Daily Tracking (10 minutes)**
- Update your CRM or spreadsheet
- Know your numbers

**Miss these? Your pipeline dries up. Revenue stops. Cashflow (your oxygen from Section 3) disappears.**

### The Follow-Up Framework

Research shows 80% of sales require 5+ follow-up contacts. Most salespeople give up after 1-2.

Here's a follow-up cadence that works:

| Day | Action | Purpose |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **Day 1** | Thank you + value recap | Cement the conversation |
| **Day 3** | Share relevant resource | Add value, stay visible |
| **Day 7** | Check in call | Personal touch |
| **Day 14** | Case study or testimonial | Social proof |
| **Day 21** | Direct ask | Move to decision |
| **Day 30** | "Breakup" email | Create urgency or close file |

The "breakup" email: "I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back. I'm guessing now isn't the right time. I'll close your file for now, but feel free to reach out when things change."

This email gets responses. People hate closing loops. Give them permission to say no, and many will re-engage.

## SECTION 4 SUMMARY: SALES WITH INTEGRITY

You've now learned the foundations of integrity-first selling. Let's consolidate.

### What You Now Know

**1. Sales is problem-solving, not manipulation**
When you approach every conversation asking "How can I help?" instead of "How can I close?", everything changes. The pressure disappears. The manipulation becomes unnecessary. You're just two people figuring out if you can help each other.

**2. The 7 principles of influence are tools, not tricks**
Reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity, and unity describe how humans make decisions. You can use them ethically to help people make good choices, or unethically to exploit them. Your intent determines the ethics.

**3. Your archetype determines your natural sales style**
Rulers sell with authority. Creators sell with vision. Sages sell with wisdom. Warriors sell with results. Caregivers sell with empathy. Stop fighting your nature. Leverage it.

**4. Objections are questions, not rejections**
When someone objects, they're asking for more information. Acknowledge, clarify, respond. Don't argue. Don't pressure. But don't give up at the first sign of resistance either.

**5. Closing is confirming, not conquering**
The close should feel like the natural conclusion of a good conversation. Assumptive, trial, alternative, summary—choose approaches that match your style and honor the prospect.

**6. What gets tracked gets improved**
Know your pipeline value, conversion rates, sales cycle, and daily activities. Professionals track. Amateurs guess.
