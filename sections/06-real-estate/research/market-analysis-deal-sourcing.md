## ULTRATHINK Section: Deep Reasoning on Market Analysis

### Why Did Toby Coach Students to Avoid Certain Markets?

**Market Selection is the First Deal Decision**

The best analysis skills in the world can't save you in the wrong market. Toby's coaching emphasizes market selection BEFORE deal sourcing because:

1. **Market velocity determines exit certainty**
   - Fast-moving markets = quick sales, predictable timelines
   - Slow markets = holding cost risk, price discovery issues

2. **Price point affects buyer pool**
   - <$150k markets have cash buyer competition (wholesalers, investors)
   - $200-400k markets have retail buyer depth
   - >$500k markets have fewer buyers, longer selling times

3. **Economic fundamentals drive sustainability**
   - Single-employer towns are risky (plant closure = market crash)
   - Diversified economies absorb shocks better
   - Population growth markets outperform declining areas

### The "Sherman-Denison Problem" â€” Toby's Texas Market Case Study

**What Happened:**
Sherman-Denison, Texas appeared attractive to investors:
- Low entry prices ($80-120k properties)
- Decent rent-to-value ratios
- Northern DFW spillover expected

**The Hidden Problem:**
- Limited economic diversity (heavily dependent on few employers)
- Thin buyer pool (few retail buyers for flips)
- High investor competition (everyone saw same "opportunity")
- Slow appreciation despite DFW proximity
- Exit times stretched from 30 days to 90+ days

**Toby's Warning Signs:**
"When you see lots of investors but not many retail buyers, you're competing for the same exit. The market looks good on paper, but when you go to sell, you're selling to other investorsâ€”and they want deals too. You can't both get a deal."

**Lesson:** Analyze WHO is buying, not just WHAT is selling.

### What Market Signals Indicate "Too Hot" vs. "Opportunity"?

**"Too Hot" Market Signals (Danger Zone):**
- Days on market <10 for any decent property
- Multiple offers on every listing (5+ common)
- Investors waiving inspections to compete
- Appraisals consistently coming in below contract
- Rent-to-price ratios below 0.5% monthly
- "Guru" courses featuring the market
- National media coverage ("hottest market!")

**"Opportunity" Market Signals:**
- Days on market 20-45 (balanced)
- 2-4 offers on good properties (competitive but manageable)
- Deals still possible with proper due diligence
- Rent-to-price ratios 0.8-1.2% monthly
- Population growth 1-3% annually
- Job growth positive, diversified employers
- Infrastructure investments announced
- Not yet "discovered" by national investors

**"Distressed/Declining" Market Signals (Avoid):**
- Days on market >90 average
- Few buyers at any price point
- Population declining
- Major employer layoffs or closures
- Government/institutional selling (foreclosures spiking)
- Rents declining
- High vacancy rates

### How Do You Identify Emerging Markets Before They Peak?

**The Leading Indicator Framework:**

**Stage 1: Pre-Discovery (Best Time to Enter)**
- Population growth just starting (1-2%)
- New employers announcing
- Infrastructure projects funded
- Still affordable to median income
- Few investors aware
- Media coverage: None

**Stage 2: Early Discovery (Good Entry)**
- Population growth accelerating (2-3%)
- Job growth visible in data
- First "this market is interesting" articles
- Prices rising but still below neighboring markets
- Investor interest growing
- Media coverage: Trade publications

**Stage 3: Mainstream Discovery (Caution)**
- Population growth strong (3%+)
- Multiple national employers arrived
- "Hottest market" articles appearing
- Prices catching up to comparable markets
- Heavy investor activity
- Media coverage: National news

**Stage 4: Peak/Saturation (Danger)**
- Population growth may slow (migration slowing)
- All the good deals require top-dollar
- "This time is different" mentality
- Prices exceed fundamentals
- Investors competing fiercely
- Media coverage: HGTV shows, guru courses

**Stage 5: Correction (Wait for Opportunity)**
- Prices declining or flat
- Investors fleeing
- "Market is dead" narrative
- Deals emerge as holders capitulate
- Cash buyers win
- Media coverage: "Market crash" stories

## Executive Summary

Market analysis and deal sourcing are the twin skills that feed every successful real estate business. Understanding where to invest (market selection) and how to find deals (sourcing) creates sustainable competitive advantage. This research provides comprehensive frameworks for analyzing markets, sourcing deals through multiple channels, and building relationships that generate consistent deal flow.

The core principle: **The best investors find the best markets AND the best deals within those markets.**

### 2. Neighborhood Valuation Methodology

#### Price-Per-Square-Foot Trend Analysis

**Why $/Sq Ft Matters:**
- Normalizes comparisons across different sized properties
- Shows true market direction (up, down, flat)
- Identifies neighborhoods outperforming or underperforming
- Reveals where to buy (below trend) and where to avoid (above trend)

**How to Calculate $/Sq Ft Trends:**

```
Step 1: Pull sold data for target neighborhood (90-180 days)
Step 2: Calculate $/sq ft for each sale
Step 3: Calculate average $/sq ft
Step 4: Compare to:
   - Same neighborhood 1 year ago
   - Same neighborhood 2 years ago
   - Adjacent/competing neighborhoods now
   - Market overall average

EXAMPLE:
Neighborhood: Oak Hills, Dallas suburb

Current average $/sq ft: $145
1 year ago: $138
2 years ago: $125

Year-over-year change: +5.1%
Two-year change: +16%
Market overall: +4% YoY

Analysis: Oak Hills appreciating faster than market average.
Consider: Is this sustainable? What's driving it?
```

### 3. Deal Sourcing Strategies

#### Source 1: MLS (Multiple Listing Service)

**Pros:**
- Largest inventory of available properties
- Clear pricing data
- Professional representation
- Structured transaction process

**Cons:**
- Most competitive channel
- Full retail or near-retail pricing
- Slower deal flow
- Agent commissions add cost

**MLS Deal Finding Strategies:**

1. **Days on Market Filter**
   - Target: 45+ DOM listings
   - Why: Motivated sellers, price reduction likely
   - Action: Make below-asking offers, be patient

2. **Price Reduction Alerts**
   - Target: Properties with 2+ reductions
   - Why: Seller motivation increasing
   - Action: Track reduction pattern, time your offer

3. **Failed Pending Alert**
   - Target: Properties back on market after pending
   - Why: Seller frustrated, deal may have fallen through for fixable reason
   - Action: Ask listing agent what happened, make clean offer

4. **REO/Bank-Owned**
   - Target: Foreclosure properties on MLS
   - Why: Banks want to move inventory
   - Action: Submit through asset manager, be patient with process

5. **Estate Sales**
   - Target: Listings mentioning estate, probate, inherited
   - Why: Heirs often want quick sale, may price below market
   - Action: Move quickly, offer simplicity

#### Source 2: Wholesalers

**Pros:**
- Pre-negotiated deals
- Often off-market opportunities
- Wholesale pricing (below market)
- Deal flow without marketing cost

**Cons:**
- Wholesale fee adds cost
- Quality varies significantly
- ARVs often inflated
- Repair estimates often low

**Building Wholesaler Relationships:**

1. **Find Active Wholesalers:**
   - Attend local REI meetups
   - Join Facebook investor groups
   - Search "we buy houses" + [your city]
   - Ask other investors who they use

2. **Evaluate Wholesaler Quality:**
   - How long in business?
   - How many deals closed monthly?
   - References from other buyers?
   - How do they calculate ARV and repairs?

3. **Set Clear Criteria:**
   - Provide written buying criteria
   - Be specific: Price range, neighborhoods, property types
   - Communicate response timeline expectations

4. **Build Preference Status:**
   - Close deals you commit to
   - Respond quickly to opportunities
   - Give honest feedback on rejected deals
   - Pay on time, every time

**Wholesaler Deal Evaluation Checklist:**

```

â–¡ Confirm assignment fee is reasonable (<$10k typical)

â–¡ Confirm seller awareness of assignment

### 4. Relationship Building Protocols

#### Building Agent Relationships

**What Agents Want from Investors:**
- Close deals (don't waste their time)
- Quick decisions (respond fast to listings)
- Easy transactions (financing in place)
- Repeat business (loyalty)
- Referrals (other investors, buyers)

**How to Become an Agent's Favorite Investor:**

1. **Be Specific About Criteria**
   - Exact neighborhoods
   - Price range (firm)
   - Property types
   - Deal breakers

2. **Respond Quickly**
   - Within 1 hour during business hours
   - Clear yes/no/maybe with reasoning
   - Don't ghost on sent listings

3. **Close What You Commit To**
   - Don't back out without valid reason
   - Honor contracts
   - Be reasonable in negotiations

4. **Make Their Job Easy**
   - Proof of funds ready
   - Pre-approval letters updated
   - Contractor already lined up
   - Can close quickly

5. **Show Appreciation**
   - Thank them for deals (even rejected ones)
   - Send referrals their way
   - Small gifts after closings

#### Building Wholesaler Relationships

**What Wholesalers Want:**
- Quick response to deals
- Honest feedback on rejected deals
- Buyers who close
- Repeat business
- Referrals to other buyers

**Becoming a Preferred Buyer:**

1. **Respond to Every Deal Within 24 Hours**
   - Even if passing, acknowledge receipt
   - Provide specific reason for passing
   - "ARV looks 10% high" or "Neighborhood doesn't work for me"

2. **Close Quickly**
   - Have financing ready
   - Don't extend inspection periods unnecessarily
   - Wire funds on time

3. **Be Known as "Easy to Work With"**
   - Don't nickel-and-dime after contract
   - Handle issues professionally
   - Communicate proactively

4. **Provide Honest Feedback**
   - Help wholesalers learn what works
   - Specific feedback improves their deal quality
   - They'll send better deals your way

TARGET STATE (6 months):
Wholesalers: 40% (still use, but selective)
MLS: 25% (build agent relationships)
Direct mail: 20% (create own deal flow)
Driving/D2S: 10% (supplement)
Network: 5% (relationships)

IMPLEMENTATION:
Month 1-2: Add 2 investor-friendly agents, set criteria
Month 2-3: Launch 500-piece direct mail test campaign
Month 3-4: Start driving for dollars 3 hours/week
Month 4-5: Attend 2 REI events, build network
Month 6: Evaluate, adjust percentages
```

**Outcome (6 months later):**
- Student closed 3 deals from agents (better margins than wholesaler deals)
- Direct mail generating 3-5 leads monthly
- One deal from driving for dollars (best margin yet)
- Assignment fee savings: Average $4k/deal (no middleman)
- Overall profitability up 35%

### Case Study 3: The "Too Hot" Market Exit

**Situation:** Student had been successful in Austin market 2019-2021. By late 2022, finding deals became nearly impossible.

**Market Indicators:**
- DOM: <7 days for anything decent
- Multiple offers: 10-15 offers common
- Investors waiving inspections to compete
- Prices exceeding sustainable levels
- Appraisals regularly below contract

**Toby's Guidance:**
"Austin isn't a deal market anymoreâ€”it's a speculation market. You're not finding deals, you're hoping appreciation bails you out. That's gambling. Two options: (1) Expand to San Antonio or secondary markets, (2) Shift strategy to off-market only with creative financing."

**Student's Pivot:**
- Shifted 70% of focus to San Antonio market
- Kept Austin presence but only for off-market/creative deals
- Developed direct-to-seller marketing in SA
- Accepted lower appreciation potential for better margins

**Lesson:** Markets cycle. Adapt strategy to market conditions; don't force it.

## Recommended Exercises

### Exercise 1: Complete Market Scorecard for Your Target Market

**Purpose:** Apply the ASCEND Market Scorecard to your investment area

**Method:**
1. Select your primary target market
2. Gather data for all scorecard metrics
3. Score each factor honestly (don't inflate)
4. Calculate total score
5. Compare to 2-3 alternative markets
6. Document your analysis and conclusion

**Time Required:** 4-6 hours per market
**Tools Needed:** Zillow, Census data, BLS, local MLS

---

### Exercise 2: Build Your Agent Prospecting List

**Purpose:** Identify and begin relationships with investor-friendly agents

**Method:**
1. Search for "investor friendly real estate agent" + your city
2. Ask at local REI meetups for recommendations
3. Check BiggerPockets forums for agent referrals
4. Create list of 10 potential agents
5. Interview 5 of them (use questions provided)
6. Select 2-3 to work with actively

**Time Required:** 3-4 hours
**Goal:** 2-3 agent relationships established within 30 days

---

### Exercise 3: Launch Direct Mail Test Campaign

**Purpose:** Test direct mail marketing in your market

**Method:**
1. Select one list type (start with: absentee owners, high equity)
2. Purchase 500 records for your target area
3. Create simple mail piece (yellow letter or postcard)
4. Set up tracking phone number
5. Mail entire list
6. Track responses for 30 days
7. Analyze: Response rate, lead quality, cost per lead
8. Adjust and repeat or abandon based on results

**Time Required:** 4-6 hours setup; ongoing response handling
**Budget:** $300-750 for 500-piece test

---

### Exercise 4: Drive for Dollars Documentation

**Purpose:** Practice identifying distressed properties and building pipeline

**Method:**
1. Select 3 target neighborhoods
2. Drive each neighborhood for 1 hour
3. Document every distressed property found
4. Skip trace 20 of the most distressed
5. Send direct mail piece to all 20
6. Make phone call attempt to 10
7. Track responses and results
8. Calculate: Time invested vs leads generated

**Time Required:** 4-5 hours total
**Tools:** DealMachine app (optional), skip trace service

---

### Exercise 5: Wholesaler Evaluation and Ranking

**Purpose:** Systematically evaluate wholesalers in your market

**Method:**
1. Identify 5-10 active wholesalers in your market
2. Get on their buyer's list
3. Evaluate 3 deals from each over 60 days
4. For each deal, verify: ARV accuracy, repair accuracy, assignment fee
5. Rank wholesalers by deal quality
6. Focus relationship on top 2-3

**Time Required:** Ongoing over 60 days
**Outcome:** Curated wholesaler relationships

## Key Takeaways

1. **Market Selection Comes First:** The best deal analysis can't fix a bad market choice
2. **Use Multiple Data Sources:** Cross-reference economic data with housing data with local knowledge
3. **Diversify Deal Sources:** Don't rely 100% on any single channel
4. **Build Relationships Before Deals:** Agents, wholesalers, and network take time to develop
5. **Monitor Market Cycles:** What works today may not work tomorrowâ€”stay adaptive
6. **Quality Over Quantity:** 10 well-qualified leads beats 100 random contacts
7. **Direct-to-Seller Wins:** Creating your own deal flow removes competition
8. **Track Everything:** Know your cost per lead, cost per deal, and source performance
9. **Trust But Verify:** Always verify wholesaler numbers with your own analysis
10. **Local Knowledge Compounds:** Deep knowledge of fewer areas beats shallow knowledge of many
