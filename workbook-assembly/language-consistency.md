# Language Consistency Matrix
## The Unshakable Empire Workbook - Terminology & Phrasing Standards

This document establishes consistent language across all 9 sections, ensuring unified voice, branded terminology, and precise usage of key concepts.

## Core Terminology Reference

### Primary Brand Terms

| Term | Definition | Usage Notes | NEVER Say |
|------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Unshakable** | The ultimate identity state - resilient, grounded, unmoved by circumstances | Capitalize when referring to the identity state; lowercase for general description | "Unbreakable" (implies no bending), "Indestructible" (implies no damage) |
| **Empire** | A business/life structure that multiplies, persists beyond the individual, and creates legacy | Always implies systems, team, and generational thinking | "Business" when meaning scaled system; "Kingdom" without faith context |
| **The Unshakable Empire** | The complete vision - identity + systems + legacy | Full phrase used at key moments, not every mention | Shortening to just "Unshakable" or just "Empire" in formal references |

### Identity & Transformation Language

| Term | Correct Usage | Context | Incorrect Alternatives |
|------|--------------|---------|------------------------|
| **"I am becoming..."** | Always future-progressive; includes the ellipsis | Declaration statements throughout | "I am..." (too absolute), "I will be..." (too distant), "I want to be..." (weak) |
| **Identity** | Who you ARE, not what you DO | Foundation concept | "Goals," "Aspirations," "Dreams" (these are different) |
| **Transformation** | Ongoing process of becoming | Journey framing | "Change" (too passive), "Improvement" (too incremental) |
| **Declaration** | Spoken/written statement of identity | Exercises, commitments | "Affirmation" (too passive), "Goal" (action, not identity) |
| **Growth Mindset** | Belief that abilities can be developed | Section 1, referenced throughout | "Positive thinking" (not the same) |
| **Fixed Mindset** | Belief that abilities are static | Contrast concept | "Negative thinking" (different concept) |

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Overall Voice Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description | Example |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | Clear, no hedging | "Do this" not "You might want to consider..." |
| **Empowering** | Reader has agency | "You choose" not "You should" |
| **Experienced** | Earned wisdom | "I learned" not "Studies show" (use Toby's voice) |
| **Warm but Challenging** | Supportive push | "This is hard AND you can do it" |
| **Action-Oriented** | Bias toward action | "Start now" not "Think about starting" |

### Tone Progression by Section

| Section | Primary Tone | Secondary Tone |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | Foundational, hopeful | Challenging limiting beliefs |
| 2 | Energetic, activating | Accountable, standards-setting |
| 3 | Clarifying, empowering | Practical, no-nonsense |
| 4 | Confident, encouraging | Rejection-normalizing |
| 5 | Strategic, systematic | Visionary, inspiring |
| 6 | Grounded, analytical | Cautionary (leverage risks) |
| 7 | Authoritative, expansive | Servant-leadership modeling |
| 8 | Warm, meaningful | Urgent (legacy time) |
| 9 | Intense, honest | Ultimately hopeful |

### Point of View

- **Second Person (you)** - Primary for instructions, exercises
- **First Person (I)** - Toby's story sections, declarations
- **First Person Plural (we)** - Occasional for journey together
- **NEVER** - Third person distant ("One should...")

## Writing Conventions

### Capitalization Rules

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Section Titles | Title Case | "Mind & Identity" |
| Chapter Titles | Title Case | "The Foundation of Identity" |
| Headers | Title Case (H1-H2), Sentence case (H3) | "Building Your Empire" / "How to start" |
| Branded Phrases | Capitalize When Quoted | "Speed is Currency" |
| Terms with Specific Meaning | Capitalize | "Daily Disciplines," "Non-Negotiables" |
| General terms | Lowercase | "the disciplines you practice" |
| Frameworks/Acronyms | All Caps | "OODA Loop," "RACI" |

### Punctuation Standards

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Ellipsis** | Three dots, space after | "I am becoming... stronger" |
| **Em Dash** | No spaces around | "This is hard—really hard" |
| **Lists** | Parallel construction | "Start, build, scale" not "Starting, build, to scale" |
| **Quotes** | Double quotes for direct | "Speed is Currency" |
| **Contractions** | Use sparingly for warmth | "Don't wait" acceptable |

### Number Rules

| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| One through nine | Spell out | "three disciplines" |
| 10 and above | Numerals | "12 exercises" |
| Percentages | Numerals + % | "10% of income" |
| Money | $ + numerals | "$600" |
| Time periods | Spell small, numerals large | "seven days," "90 days" |

## Cross-Reference Language Templates

### Referring Back

Use these phrases when referencing earlier content:

- "In Section X, you established..."
- "Remember your declaration: 'I am becoming...'"
- "This builds on Exercise X.X where you..."
- "Apply the [framework] from Section X"
- "Your [document] from Section X becomes relevant here"

### Looking Forward

Use these phrases when teasing future content:

- "In Section X, we'll build on this foundation..."
- "This prepares you for [topic] in Section X"
- "Keep this work—you'll need it in Section X"
- "The crisis section will test this skill"

### Connection Phrases

- "This is where [Theme] meets [Theme]"
- "Notice how [concept] connects to [concept]"
- "The principle of [principle] applies here too"

## Prohibited Language

### Terms to Never Use

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "Easy" | False promise | "Simple but not easy" |
| "Quick fix" | Against philosophy | "Foundational change" |
| "Get rich quick" | Opposite of message | "Build wealth" |
| "Guarantee" | Can't promise outcomes | "Principles that work" |
| "Secrets" | Sensationalist | "Principles," "Frameworks" |
| "Hack" | Trivializes | "Strategy," "Approach" |
| "Guru" | Self-aggrandizing | "Guide," "Mentor" |
| "Crush it" | Bro culture | "Execute," "Achieve" |
| "Hustle" alone | Implies unsustainable | "Disciplined work" |
| "Passive" without caveat | Nothing is purely passive | "Leveraged," "Systematized" |

### Phrases to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| "Just do it" | Oversimplifies |
| "Fake it till you make it" | Against identity authenticity |
| "Work-life balance" | Implies separation |
| "The secret to success" | Clickbait language |
| "You should..." | Preachy, removes agency |
| "Obviously" / "Clearly" | Condescending |
| "As mentioned before" | Weak cross-reference |
