## Reinforcement Loops

These concept pairs STRENGTHEN each other when taught together:

1. **Identity ↔ Crisis Resilience** (Sections 1 & 9)
   - Strong identity enables crisis navigation
   - Crisis tests and strengthens identity

2. **Daily Disciplines ↔ Financial Success** (Sections 2 & 3)
   - Disciplines enable wealth building
   - Financial success validates discipline

3. **Sales ↔ Business Growth** (Sections 4 & 5)
   - Sales feeds business
   - Business systems multiply sales

4. **Real Estate ↔ Generational Wealth** (Sections 6 & 8)
   - RE builds legacy assets
   - Legacy thinking guides RE decisions

5. **Empire Systems ↔ Family Systems** (Sections 7 & 8)
   - Team leadership skills transfer to family
   - Family purpose fuels team building

## Workbook Assembly Implications

### Sequencing Requirements
1. Section 1 MUST be first (all foundations)
2. Section 2 MUST follow Section 1 (execution requires identity)
3. Section 9 SHOULD be last (tests everything)
4. Sections 3-8 can have flexible ordering BUT dependencies noted above must be respected

### Cross-Reference Requirements
- Each section should reference relevant concepts from earlier sections
- Later sections should include "refresh" reminders of foundational concepts
- Crisis section should explicitly tie back to ALL previous sections

### Exercise Dependencies
- Advanced exercises should require completion of prerequisite section exercises
- Some exercises should span multiple sections
- Crisis exercises should integrate elements from ALL sections
