## **2. Foundational Stories That Teach**

These stories are frequently used to illustrate key principles and life lessons.

### **The 2008 Crash and Rebuilding**
Toby built a massive real estate empire in the early 2000s, including a mortgage company, a RE/MAX franchise, a title company, and an appraisal company. In the 2008 financial crash, over 252 national lenders failed, and he "lost it all." This experience of being completely wiped out and having to rebuild from scratch is the crucible that forged his philosophy on risk management, the importance of a solid personal foundation (spiritual, mental, physical), and the resilience required to succeed. It's the ultimate proof that you can come back from anything if you refuse to quit.

### **The "Realtor Test"**
When a realtor presented a deal with an inflated After Repair Value (ARV), Toby challenged them: "If you're so confident it will sell for that price, put your commission in escrow. If it sells for your number, I'll double it. If it doesn't, you get nothing." The realtor refused. This story teaches the importance of challenging assumptions, demanding that partners have "skin in the game," and not trusting valuations that aren't backed by data and conviction.

### **The First Deal with Zig Ziglar's Book**
After facing a dead end in his career path, a mentor (Bill Langley) gave Toby the book "See You at the Top" by Zig Ziglar and told him not to come back until he'd read it. The book resonated so deeply that it gave him the clarity and framework to map out a new future. He created a detailed plan to move to Dallas and join the police department. His mentor was so impressed by the plan that he wrote Toby a check for $1,500 to help him succeed. This story highlights the transformative power of mentorship, finding the right knowledge at the right time, and creating a detailed, actionable plan.

### **The "Embarrassing Offer" Strategy**
Toby often makes aggressive, low-ball offers to brokers on properties, sometimes even telling them not to present the offer if it would "embarrass" them. He notes that in about 50% of cases, the deal comes back to him 6-10 months later after other buyers with more optimistic numbers have failed to close. This teaches the value of patience, sticking to your numbers, and understanding that time often makes sellers more realistic.

### **The Prayer for a Partner**
While mowing a yard late one night, feeling ready to settle down, Toby prayed a very specific prayer for his future wife. He asked for a brunette country girl who would be a true partner in life and business. Shortly after, he met Angie, a brunette barrel racer, under circumstances orchestrated by his roommate. Despite a rocky start, he knew instantly she was the one and relentlessly pursued her for 30 days until she agreed to a date. They have now been married for 30 years. This story underscores his belief in faith, vision, and the power of knowing exactly what you want and pursuing it without quit.

## **3. The Three-Legged Stool Foundation**

The cornerstone of Toby's transformation philosophy—all three legs must be strong for sustainable success:

### **1. Spiritual Authority**
Begin each day with a focus on gratitude, reverence, and a clear sense of purpose. Align decisions with core values to stay grounded. This isn't about religion but about connecting to something greater than yourself and operating from a foundation of integrity and purpose.

### **2. Mental Mastery**
Guard your thoughts, filter the information you consume, and build an environment that nurtures clarity and decisiveness. This includes controlling inputs, maintaining focus, and developing the ability to think clearly under pressure.

### **3. Physical Stewardship**
Treat your body as the foundation of all performance—prioritize proper nutrition, movement, and recovery to sustain long-term output. Physical health directly impacts mental clarity and spiritual connection.

## **4. Core Mindset Principles**

1. **Discipline Over Motivation:** Motivation is fleeting; disciplined habits create compounding progress.
2. **Control What You Can:** Interest rates, market cycles, and competition are outside your control—your effort, preparation, and focus are not.
3. **Resilience is a Muscle:** It strengthens when tested by setbacks, delays, and adversity.
4. **Adapt or Die:** Clinging to one approach in a changing market is a fast route to failure; adaptability is survival.
5. **Commitment First, Clarity Second:** Begin before you feel fully ready, and let momentum create clarity.
6. **Small Wins Stack Big Results:** Celebrate measurable daily progress to maintain long-term drive.
7. **Self-Mastery Equals Market Mastery:** The investor who can manage their emotions, energy, and focus will outlast those who can't.
8. **Choose Challenge:** Seek discomfort regularly so unexpected challenges don't knock you out.

## **5. Power Concepts for Transformation**

### **Inversion**
Before stacking new habits, identify and remove the small daily compromises that undermine progress. Sometimes subtraction is more powerful than addition.

### **Action Creates Clarity**
Waiting for perfect certainty is paralysis—act decisively, and allow momentum to refine your path. Movement creates momentum, momentum creates clarity.

### **Second Audit**
A deeper self-assessment requiring hard evidence of progress—visible in your behavior, metrics, and results. Go beyond surface-level self-evaluation.

### **Comfort is a Cage**
Growth only exists outside the boundaries of comfort; every breakthrough comes with discomfort. Embrace the suck.

### **Identity Before Outcome**
Redefine yourself at the level of identity first, so actions naturally align with that new self-image. Be-Do-Have, not Have-Do-Be.

## **7. Daily Mental Habits & Practices**

### **Morning Reset**
Begin with gratitude, intentional breathing, and your top priorities for the day. Set the tone before the world sets it for you.

### **Rule of Three**
Identify and focus on the three most important actions that will move the needle. Clarity comes from constraint.

### **Evening Review**
End each day with reflection—ask "What worked? What didn't? What will I improve tomorrow?" Measure to manage.

### **Guard Inputs**
Be intentional about what you read, watch, and listen to, avoiding negativity and distractions. You become what you consume.

### **Physical Priming**
Start your day with movement to energize your mind. Motion creates emotion.

### **Written Commitments**
Keep a visible list of commitments to yourself and others. What gets written gets done.

## **8. The IGNITE Program Weekly Flow**

### **Week 1 — Establish Command**
Remove distractions, declutter your environment, and lay the foundation for discipline. Create the conditions for success.

### **Week 2 — Forge Vision**
Craft a vivid, detailed image of your future self, calculate the real cost of getting there, and connect it emotionally to your why. Vision without emotion is just a wish.

### **Week 3 — Self-Audit**
Examine your integrity, ownership, and consistency; confront gaps without excuses. Truth-telling is the first step to change.

### **Week 4 — Protocol**
Lock in sustainable systems—morning reset, evening shutdown, accountability structures, and a written personal constitution. Systems beat willpower.

## **9. Handling Pressure & Setbacks**

### **Emotional Regulation**
Pause, breathe, and assess before responding to challenges. Response vs. reaction is the difference between amateurs and pros.

### **Reframe Failures**
See them as valuable tuition in your education as an investor and leader. Every failure is data.

### **Scenario Planning**
Always have contingency plans for worst-case outcomes. Hope for the best, prepare for the worst.

### **Physical Health Link**
Recognize that mental stamina is fueled by physical health. You can't think clearly when your body is breaking down.

### **Mentor Perspective**
Consult trusted advisors to gain clarity and avoid reactive decisions. Outside perspective beats inside emotion.

### **Chunk Down Problems**
Break overwhelming challenges into smaller, solvable steps. Eat the elephant one bite at a time.
